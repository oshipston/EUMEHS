The University of Edinburgh



The University of Edinburgh is a globally renowned education and research institute. Founded in 1582, the university has played host to some of modern societyâ€™s philosophical and literary paragons for over 400 years with alumni including: Sir Arthur Conan Doyle, J.M. Barrie, Charles Darwin and Robert Louis Stevenson.


Today Edinburgh University encompasses over 25,000 undergraduate and 14,000 post-graduate students. It is comprised of over 4000 full-time academic faculty spread over several urban and peripheral campuses. It is ranked 19th in the QS world university rankings and is seated in the heart of Edinburgh, a cultural and international hub whose famous festival season each summer brings over 500,000 visitors and more than 2000 performers.


Not only a cultural center, Edinburgh university is also home to one of the oldest medical schools in the world, founded in 1726. Origin of the Edinburgh model of medical education, the school has produced graduates that have gone on to found medical colleges around the world including Harvard, Yale, Imperial and University College London.


We as a society hope to capitalise upon the depth of medical and literary history that the University of Edinburgh provides and cultivate a place for people to further explore humanities in a medical and humanist context.
