The Committee


Chairperson


Haris Haseeb

"Hi! I’m Haris, a fifth year medical student here in Edinburgh. Starting the EUMEHS initiative has been both exciting and rewarding. For a while now, I’ve maintained that the study of medicine is fundamentally interdisciplinary, and throughout medical school, I’ve committed my academic research into exploring this. Intercalating in Medicine and Literature was invaluable, and this year, I’m dedicating more time into studying key periods in the history of medicine; specifically, the Romantic period and Islamic renaissance. I guess I hope that, in doing so, I might be better equipped to deal with the massive uncertainties which oppose medical science today."


Vice-Chairperson


Angharad Campbell

"I'm a fifth year medical student at Edinburgh, and feel very lucky to be Vice-Chairperson for EUMEHS this year. I did my Intercalated degree in Global Health Policy; an area full of ethical debates and dilemmas. I'm currently conducting a research project with, particularly, drug overdose patients and have taken a particular interest in ethical research practises. "The Immortal Life of Henrietta Lacks" is my favourite book, and in second year I organised a project to diagnose a fictional patient in a French novel using modern psychiatry techniques. I'm excited to see where the society will take us this year - there's lots in the pipeline!"


Secretary


Robyn Canham

"My name is Robyn Canham and I am in my final year of medicine at the University of Edinburgh. I am delighted to be continuing in the role of secretary for the EUMEHS for the upcoming academic year. I have an interest in medical ethics, with a particular interest in how ethics is taught within the medical curriculum; having undertaken a project looking into how perspectives of medical ethics teaching differ between medical students and practising junior doctors. I also have a keen interest in medical history, so feel very lucky to be studying in a city with such a rich and colourful medical past, which I hope can continue to inspire its current students. We have laid the foundations for what should be an exciting second year and I am looking forward to watching our society flourish."

Clinical Committee Member


Kana Yaso

"Hi, I'm Kanastana Yasotharan, a fifth year medical student at University of Edinburgh and the clinical year General Committee Member of EUMEHS. This is my second year living in Edinburgh after graduating from the University of St Andrews as a Bachelor of Medicine in 2016. As a Sri Lankan Tamil-Canadian studying in Scotland I am continually learning about culture and identity and the means of which our environment influences our perceptions and experiences. I had the honour and privilege to spend the summer working at the Palliative Care Unit at Baycrest Hospital in Toronto, Ontario where I was constantly compelled to contemplate the ethics and humanities in medicine. I am a mental health advocate, intersectional feminist and believe in a holistic approach to delivering medicine."
